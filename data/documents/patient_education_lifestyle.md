# Living Well with Heart Failure: Patient Education and Lifestyle

Daily habits make a real difference in how people with HFpEF and related
cardio-kidney-metabolic conditions feel and how often they end up in the
hospital. The steps below support, but do not replace, the plan from your own
healthcare team.

## Track your weight

Weigh yourself at the same time each morning, after using the bathroom and
before breakfast, using the same scale. Sudden weight gain often means the body
is holding extra fluid. A common guideline is to call your care team if you gain
more than about 2 to 3 pounds (1 to 1.5 kg) in a day, or about 5 pounds (2.3 kg)
in a week, but follow the specific numbers your team gives you.

## Watch your salt and fluids

- **Sodium (salt):** Eating less salt helps prevent fluid buildup. Many care
  teams suggest limiting sodium, often to around 2,000 mg per day. Read labels
  and limit processed foods, canned soups, and restaurant meals, which are often
  very high in salt.
- **Fluids:** Some people are asked to limit how much they drink each day. Ask
  your team whether a fluid limit applies to you.

## Stay active

Regular, moderate activity such as walking improves energy and quality of life.
Start slowly, build up gradually, and ask your care team about a cardiac
rehabilitation program. Stop and rest if you feel chest pain, severe
breathlessness, or dizziness, and report these symptoms.

## Take medicines as prescribed

Take all medicines exactly as directed, even on days you feel well. Do not stop
or change doses without talking to your care team. Use a pill organizer or phone
reminders if that helps. Bring an up-to-date medicine list to every appointment.

## Manage related conditions

Keep blood pressure, blood sugar, and cholesterol within the targets your team
sets. Attend appointments for diabetes, kidney, and blood-pressure care, since
these conditions all affect the heart.

## Other healthy habits

- Do not smoke, and ask for help quitting if you do.
- Limit or avoid alcohol, following your team's advice.
- Aim for good sleep, and ask about testing if you snore heavily or stop
  breathing during sleep (possible sleep apnea).
- Get recommended vaccinations, such as the yearly flu shot, to lower the risk
  of infections that can worsen heart failure.

## Know your normal

Learn the symptoms that are usual for you so you can notice changes early.
Keeping a simple daily log of weight, symptoms, and blood pressure can help you
and your care team spot problems before they become serious.
