# Safety Warnings and When to Get Help

This guide explains warning signs for people living with HFpEF and related
heart, kidney, and metabolic conditions. It does not replace medical advice.
When in doubt, contact a healthcare professional.

## Call emergency services right away

Some symptoms can signal a life-threatening emergency. Call your local emergency
number (such as 911) immediately if you or someone else has:

- **Chest pain, pressure, or tightness**, especially if it spreads to the arm,
  neck, jaw, or back, or comes with sweating or nausea
- **Severe or sudden shortness of breath**, or breathlessness at rest that does
  not ease
- **Fainting, passing out, or loss of consciousness**
- **Severe or sudden dizziness** with a feeling that you might collapse
- **A fast or irregular heartbeat with chest pain, fainting, or breathlessness**
- **Signs of a stroke:** sudden face drooping, arm weakness, or slurred speech
- **Coughing up pink, frothy fluid**, which can signal fluid flooding the lungs

These are emergencies. Do not wait, and do not drive yourself if you can avoid
it.

## Call your care team the same day

Contact your healthcare team promptly (the same day) if you notice warning signs
that your heart failure may be worsening:

- Rapid weight gain (for example, more than 2 to 3 pounds in a day or 5 pounds
  in a week)
- New or increased swelling in the legs, ankles, or abdomen
- Increasing shortness of breath with activity, or needing more pillows to sleep
- Waking at night feeling breathless
- Feeling much more tired than usual
- A noticeable drop in how much you are urinating

## Medication safety

- Do not stop or change heart, blood-pressure, or kidney medicines on your own.
- Be cautious with over-the-counter pain relievers: nonsteroidal
  anti-inflammatory drugs (NSAIDs) such as ibuprofen and naproxen can cause
  fluid retention and harm the kidneys in people with heart failure. Ask your
  team before using them.
- Tell your care team about all supplements and herbal products, since some
  affect blood pressure, potassium, or blood thinners.
- Ask before using salt substitutes, because many contain potassium, which can
  build up to dangerous levels with certain medicines.

## Why these warnings matter

Heart failure can change quickly. Catching warning signs early often means a
simple medication adjustment instead of a hospital stay, and recognizing an
emergency early can save a life. This information is general and educational; it
is not a substitute for advice from your own doctor, nurse, or pharmacist.
